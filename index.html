Introduction

BEFORE:

Our site will be targeted at teens who live in Hong Kong and the site will tell them how to make money. The clients of the website are parents because this is a way they would want their children to learn how to be more independent and will teach them that you have to earn money.

NOW:

Our site will be targetted at AIS high school students who are looking for clubs to join. At first, I asked Srijith, the student council president to be our client, but since Ms. Kruse is the school counsellor, we later asked her instead. Ms. Kruse is looking for a user friendly website that answers most relevant questions students might ask, the website should have all the extra curricular activities on the website.

Questions

BEFORE:

1. Who will be the audience for your website?

The audience for our website will be teens (aged 13-19) living in Hong Kong trying to earn money.

2. What will be the specific purpose of your website?

The specific purpose of our website is to inspire teenagers to earn money themselves and to teach them to be more independent. Most teenagers in Hong Kong are more on the wealthy side and if they are able to visit this website (usually), then they are able to earn money by themselves.

3. Who is the client? (Whose problem will creating this website solve? Who would be willing to pay for this website?)

The client of this website is the parents of teenagers living in Hong Kong. Most parents would want their children to learn how to be independent and would want to let them know that you must earn money and that is not easy.

4. What are the objectives for the project?

  1. User friendly.
  2. Appealing to the eye.
  3. Shows doable ways of earning money (for teens).
  4. Shows how to save money.
  5. Shows different places in HK where part time jobs are needed (for older teens).
  6. Shows how to earn money at home (for younger teens).

NOW:

Since the idea of "Making Money As A Teen In Hong Kong" was directed to an audience far too big for us, we decided that we should make a website for the clubs in American International School.

1. Who will be the audience for your website?

High school students of AIS who are looking for clubs to join.

2. What will be the specific purpose of your website?

The specific purpose of the website will be for students to find a club they are interested in (and maybe even join that club).

3. Who is the client? (Whose problem will creating this website solve? Who would be willing to pay for this website?)

The client will be Ms. Kruse, the school counsellor.

4. What are the objectives for the project?

The objective is to help AIS high school students to find a club that they'll fit into and are interested in.

5. What is the success criteria? (Checklist? Rubric? Descriptors?)

Helps AIS high school students find a club that they'll fit into.


12th of January, 2015

The last few classes my group and I have been thinking of a different website to make since our audience for the previous idea was too broad. So as a group, we've decided to make a website for AIS high school students who are looking for clubs to join, which is a website with a summary of most of the high school clubs in AIS.

14th of January, 2015

Since we thought that just writing summaries of clubs would be boring to read (especially for high school students), we decided to record videos of the leaders of different clubs giving a brief description of their club and maybe get a glimps of what they do, letting the audience decide whether or not they are interested in joining that club.

I assigned different group mates to take videos of different club leaders to get work done quickly and so far, we have a few videos done. We are on task.
